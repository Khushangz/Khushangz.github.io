Johns Hopkins · CLSP
Vocal Tic Detection — Deep Dive

At Johns Hopkins University's Center for Language and Speech Processing, I am developing the first end-to-end vocal tic detection system using deep temporal modeling and speech embeddings in collaboration with clinical partners at Johns Hopkins Hospital.

The system processes raw audio into 20ms frame-level predictions, leveraging WavLM embeddings as input to a CNN-BiLSTM architecture with 12× temporal compression and 105-frame receptive field optimized for capturing tic patterns across 2.1-second windows.

I engineered a complete pipeline including preprocessing, segment alignment, padded sequence handling, and custom undersampling strategies to address severe class imbalance (98:2 non-tic to tic ratio). Through systematic experimentation with Jensen-Shannon divergence-optimized data splits, strategic undersampling, higher receptive field CNN architecture, and temporal smoothing, I improved AUROC by 80% from baseline approaches.

The model is trained and evaluated on patient-stratified intra- and inter-patient splits, achieving 0.74 AUROC despite operating at high temporal resolution with severe class imbalance. I also designed baseline experiments using SVM, VAD, MMD, and LDA to analyze tic-related signal transitions and dataset variance across patient splits, building diagnostic tools for interpretable analysis.

This cross-disciplinary effort integrates ML engineering, speech processing, and clinical insight to lay the foundation for automated tic detection systems in therapeutic and research settings.

St. Jude Children's Research Hospital
Medical Imaging AI — Deep Dive

At St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, my team and I have developed a contrastive copy-detection model aimed at identifying potential privacy breaches in publicly released medical datasets. I am leading the benchmarking of its retrieval and verification pipeline, ensuring reproducibility through consistent environments and data processing protocols.

This involves FAISS-based large-scale image retrieval, Siamese networks for patient verification, and logistic threshold-based decision functions, with evaluation metrics including patient-level bootstrap resampling, mAP@R, AUROC, precision@1, and confidence intervals. I've optimized the benchmarking pipeline for efficiency and scalability, validating the model across more than 600,000 chest X-ray images and achieving state-of-the-art accuracy with 0.89 mAP@R and 0.9 precision@1 — significantly outperforming prior approaches.

In parallel, I am working on a disease classification task using both synthetic and real-world chest X-ray datasets, training DenseNet-121 for multi-label thoracic pathology detection using FAISS-based deduplication and achieving 0.83 AUROC across 14 diseases.

Another line of work involves diffusion model inversion, where I implement and extend latent inversion pipelines using MONAI's generative framework. This includes applying DDIM-based stable diffusion inversion via null-text guidance for conditional reconstructions and generalizing it to DDPM for stochastic generation models. The inversion pipeline involves manipulating the latent space through VAE, U-Net, and noise schedulers for high-fidelity medical image reconstruction.
